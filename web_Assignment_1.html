Call of Duty: Black Ops
Call of Duty: Black Ops is a first-person shooter game set during the Cold War era. Released in 2010, it focuses on covert missions, espionage, and military operations. Players take on the role of soldiers engaged in secret missions around the globe, using a variety of weapons and technology. The game features both a gripping single-player campaign and popular multiplayer modes, including a Zombies survival mode. Known for its intense action, gripping storyline, and historical themes, Black Ops became a standout entry in the Call of Duty franchise.
DIRT Rally 2.0
Dirt Rally 2.0 is a racing video game developed and published by Codemasters for Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One. It was released on February 26, 2019. The game is the thirteenth in the Colin McRae Rally series, and the seventh in the Dirt series.
Back4Blood
Back 4 Blood is a first—person multiplayer computer game developed by Turtle Rock Studios and published by Warner Bros. Games. The game was released on Windows, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One and Xbox Series X/S on October 12, 2021.

A recent update for Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) enhanced the game's graphics and performance, featuring improved visual effects like realistic shadows and lighting, as well as updated grenade physics. The update also introduced new gameplay features, including refined hit detection and revamped maps with enhanced detail.
The God of War series will be handled by the showrunner "For the sake of all mankind"
The God of War series adaptation recently lost part of its original creative team, but Sony and Amazon quickly found a new leader. Ronald D. Moore, known for his work on "For All Mankind" and "Outlander," will take over as showrunner, executive producer, and lead writer, steering the series in a new creative direction. While Moore will shape the future of the series, key representatives from PlayStation and Vertigo, such as Cory Barlog, remain attached to the project as producers. The plot will adapt the story of the 2018 game, where an older Kratos embarks on a journey with his son Atreus to the highest mountain across the nine realms to scatter the ashes of his late wife, Faye.

The Red Dead Redemption game will be released on PC for the first time
Rockstar Games has announced the release of Red Dead Redemption (RDR) on PC. The PC version will include 4K resolution up to 144 Hz on compatible devices, support for Ultrawide and Super Ultrawide monitors, HDR10, and full mouse and keyboard support. The release is set for October 29.Originally launched on consoles in May 2010, Red Dead Redemption’s story follows former outlaw John Marston hunting down the surviving members of the Van der Linde gang. The game's description on Steam confirms English voice acting and Russian subtitles, though pricing is yet unknown.
